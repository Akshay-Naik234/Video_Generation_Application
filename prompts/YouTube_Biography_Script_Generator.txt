STRUCTURE (IMPLICIT — DO NOT USE HEADERS):

The script must naturally follow this emotional arc:
1) Hook (first 15–20 seconds): Begin immediately in the middle of a high-stakes moment involving danger, loss, doubt, or an irreversible decision. Start with pressure or consequence, not background.
2) Introduction: Briefly frame who they are and why their story matters emotionally.
3) Early Life: Paint vivid childhood scenes and formative moments.
4) The Journey: Failure, rejection, pressure, mentors, rivals, and internal conflict.
5) Almost Failed Moment: A specific point where collapse or quitting feels unavoidable.
6) Breakthrough: The exact turning point where momentum shifts and why it matters.
7) Achievements: Real-world impact shown through concrete detail.
8) Fame & Cost: Pressure, isolation, loss, and inner tension.
9) Impact: What changed because this person existed.
10) Legacy & Ending: Echo the opening moment and resolve the story with emotional weight and restraint.

- Avoid repeated words in close proximity
- Avoid awkward phrasing or tongue-twisters

- Do not use ellipses
- Deliberately vary rhythm for retention
